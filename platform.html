My Platform

An outline of my plans for the school. 

Academic

I plan to introduce a high school science fair from year 7-12, promoting student engagement in fields of Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics. This would be combined with University-backed competitions such as the Solar Car Challenge (UNSW), Tournament Of Minds (USyd), and F1 in schools (UNSW again), which engage students across all fields and learning areas. As school Captain, I would be in a position where plans for both Arts and Sciences could be implemented concurrently.

CAPA

    While the Hub is an amazing facility, the majority of the student body does not have access to the resources available. A panel of existing CAPA students, as well as members of staff, would be consulted on methods of integrating creative and performing arts across more Key Learning Areas (especially English, Religion and HSIE, which are practically impossible to isolate from creative and performing arts).
    In addition, a MADD (Music, Art, Drama and Dance) event would be hosted using the facilities and resources already available to the school, preferably biannually (twice a year). One of these events would be the school musical which, while currently postponed as a result of the global pandemic, is expected to provide a significant boost to interest in performing arts. The other would be conducted in a similar fashion to the Hub opening event in 2019, with individual performances and exhibitions from all year groups. However, this event would be thematic rather than general (i.e. all components would share a key theme), in order to promote a more cohesive and effective working environment for those involved.

    Community

    Liverpool FC is a club which heavily emphasises its fan base, both in England and abroad. With over 580 million fans worldwide, their anthem, You’ll Never Walk Alone, exemplifies what members of our school community should strive to be: members of a COMMUNITY. Liverpool fans come from all over the world, speaking hundreds of languages, and having beliefs so starkly contrasting, that it’s nearly impossible to believe that they could have anything in common. But the club promotes Unity rather than Uniformity, imploring fans to remember that, in the words of Barack Obama, “for all our outward differences, we’re in this together. We fall and rise as one,” something that is applicable not only to one of the most popular football clubs in the world, but also to a school of 2000 students in Stanhope Gardens. Events such as a MADD week will provide students from different backgrounds with the opportunity to express themselves in a way that supports the goal of unity over uniformity. 

    Student Representation

    While the student leaders at our school technically hold the position of Student Representatives, the vast majority of the student body remains under-represented, with the vast majority of subject areas being unified under the category of "learning," whilst Sports, Ministry and CAPA have independent leaders. Leaders of Learning are not able to accurately represent the needs of students, with subjects from finance to physics being categorised under the banner. In order to rectify this, the position of "Leader of Learning" should be split into 3 distinct positions: 

    • Leader for Learning: STEM,  which encompasses Technology and Applied Sciences
    • Leader for Learning: HSIE, which encompasses HSIE and Physical Education
    • Leader for Culture: which encompasses English and Religious Education

    By splitting our school's academics into three distinct categories, leaders for each learning area will be better equipped to meet the needs of students in that area. 

    To increase the effectiveness of this strategy, the executive body of the school must become more involved with the Student Representative Council. In the course of 2020, none of the proposals put forth by the SRC were adopted, or seemingly even discussed with the executive body to any meaningful extent, defeating the purpose of its existence. I propose the attendance of at least one of the school directors and/or the principal at every meeting. Seeing as meetings take a maximum of half an hour, and occur on a bi-weekly basis, the 20 hours taken up over the course of a school year will inevitably produce some meaningful result, restoring some level of significance and prestige to the Student Representative Council.
